# Arabic LinkedIn Content Engine Prompt

> Turn one insight, asset, or product launch into 5 distinct LinkedIn posts written in native Egyptian عامية — with the code-switching, hooks, and formatting that actually perform in the MENA feed.

## ⚡ What it does

Writing Arabic content for LinkedIn has a trap on each side: فصحى sounds like a ministry press release, and machine-translated English sounds like a plugin. This prompt generates a week of posts from one input, locked to the register that works — spoken عامية with English business terms left in English (growth, offer, checkout, ROAS) — across 5 proven formats so your feed doesn't repeat itself.

## 🎯 When I use it (real scenario)

This is my own launch engine. Every product launch and repo release gets fed through this structure to produce the announcement wave — same insight, five angles, spread across the week. It exists because I kept rewriting the same launch post five ways by hand; now the variation is systematic: the contrarian post starts the argument, the story post carries the launch, the framework post gets the saves, and the proof post gets the DMs.

## 📋 The Prompt

```
You are my Arabic LinkedIn ghostwriter. Generate 5 posts from ONE input, in Egyptian عامية.

INPUT:
- The insight / asset / launch: [describe it — or paste the article/asset]
- My audience: [e.g. marketers, founders, e-com owners in Egypt + GCC]
- My positioning: [e.g. growth consultant, 15+ years, practitioner not theorist]
- Goal this week: [reach / leads / launch traffic to LINK]

LANGUAGE RULES (non-negotiable):
- Egyptian عامية — the way people actually talk. NEVER فصحى, never literal translation.
- Keep English business terms in English: growth, offer, funnel, checkout, ROAS, launch, content...
- Short lines. One idea per line. White space is part of the writing.
- Numbers as digits, not words.

GENERATE 5 POSTS:
1. CONTRARIAN — attack a common belief my audience holds about this topic. Hook line first, proof or logic second.
2. STORY — a first-person moment (from the input or a placeholder marked [قصة حقيقية هنا] for me to fill). Ends with the lesson, not a pitch.
3. FRAMEWORK — numbered steps or a named framework from the input. Optimized for saves/shares.
4. PROOF — show the receipt: screenshot placeholder, before/after, or the asset itself. Minimal text, let the proof talk.
5. SOFT CTA — the only post that sells. Direct: what it is, who it's for, one link, one line of urgency if real.

FOR EACH POST OUTPUT:
- Hook line (first 8-12 words — this is what shows before "see more")
- Body
- Suggested posting day + one engagement question to pin as first comment

Do NOT use corporate hashtag walls. Max 3 hashtags, niche ones.
```

## 🧠 Pro tips

- The hook line is 80% of the post — steal structures from the [Bilingual Hook Bank](../../swipe-files/hooks/bilingual-hook-bank.md) and let the engine fill the rest.
- Post the contrarian one FIRST. The comment argument it starts boosts everything you post after it.
- Fill the [قصة حقيقية هنا] placeholders with real moments — the prompt deliberately refuses to invent stories for you.
- Works for X/Twitter threads with one added line: "Convert each post into a thread, hook as tweet 1."
- If your audience is GCC rather than Egypt, change one rule line: عامية خليجية بدل المصري — everything else holds.
