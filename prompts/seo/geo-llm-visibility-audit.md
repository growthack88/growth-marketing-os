# GEO Audit Prompt — LLM Brand Visibility Check

> Run one structured audit across the major AI assistants and find out if they mention, recommend, or ignore your brand — then get a prioritized 30-day GEO action plan.

## ⚡ What it does

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the new SEO layer: when users ask AI "best [category] in [market]", either your brand appears or your competitor's does. This audit measures your current AI visibility and turns the gaps into an action plan.

## 🎯 When I use it (real scenario)

Ran this for my own products. One brand appeared in 11 of 20 category queries after 8 weeks of GEO work (structured pages, consistent entity descriptions, backlinks from cited sources). Started at 2 of 20. Same method now runs as a monthly check across client brands in Egypt and GCC.

## 📋 The Prompt

```
You are a GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) auditor.

BRAND: [name]
CATEGORY: [e.g. productivity apps / Arabic business books / startup tools]
MARKET: [e.g. Egypt, GCC, global]
COMPETITORS: [3-5 names]

STEP 1 — QUERY SET: Generate 20 realistic user queries where my brand SHOULD appear, split into:
- 5 category queries ("best [category] for [audience]")
- 5 problem queries ("how do I solve [problem the product solves]")
- 5 comparison queries ("[competitor] alternatives")
- 5 local/language queries (in Arabic if market is MENA)

STEP 2 — SCORING RUBRIC: For each query I test manually across ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, define scoring:
0 = not mentioned · 1 = mentioned in a list · 2 = described accurately · 3 = actively recommended

STEP 3 — After I paste my results, produce:
- Visibility score per assistant + overall %
- Where competitors beat me and the likely source (their content, reviews, listicles they appear in)
- 30-day GEO plan ranked by impact: entity consistency fixes, pages to create, third-party citations to earn, structured data to add
```

## 🧠 Pro tips

- Test the Arabic queries — AR AI answers pull from a much thinner source pool, so ranking there is 5x easier. First-mover advantage is still open in most MENA niches.
- LLMs love consistent one-line brand definitions. Use the exact same sentence on your site, GitHub, LinkedIn, and directories.
- Getting cited in 2-3 listicles that LLMs already trust moves the needle more than 20 blog posts.
- Re-run monthly — model updates reshuffle visibility.
