# Campaign Postmortem Prompt

> A structured post-campaign debrief that separates decision quality from outcome luck, converts results into transferable rules, and outputs the experiment-log entry that makes your next campaign start smarter.

## ⚡ What it does

Most campaign learnings die in two ways: winners get celebrated without knowing WHY they won (so the win can't be repeated), and losers get buried without extracting the lesson (so the loss gets repeated). This prompt forces the honest middle: what did we believe going in, what actually happened at each funnel layer, which decisions were good even if the outcome was bad — and what RULE, if any, does this campaign earn the right to write?

## 🎯 When I use it (real scenario)

This became a fixed ritual for the same reason the experiment log exists in my [Growth Strategist GPT](../../gpts/growth-strategist-gpt.md) knowledge files: without written postmortems, I kept watching teams (mine included) re-test last year's failed idea with this year's budget — and re-argue settled questions from memory instead of records. Twenty minutes after each campaign closes the loop; the log becomes the collective memory that survives team changes and AI-advisor sessions alike.

## 📋 The Prompt

```
You are a performance marketing analyst running a blameless campaign postmortem. Separate decision quality from outcome luck. Be specific or be silent.

THE CAMPAIGN:
- Objective + the hypothesis going in ("we believed X would drive Y because Z"): [X]
- Budget & duration: [X]
- The setup: audience, offer, creatives, landing page: [describe]
- RESULTS (full funnel, vs target and vs account baseline):
  [spend / impressions / CTR / CPC / LP conversion / purchases-leads / CPA-ROAS]
- What we changed mid-flight, and why: [X]
- External context during the run (season, competitor moves, price changes): [X]

ANALYZE:

1. HYPOTHESIS VERDICT — was the original belief confirmed, refuted, or untested
   (campaign died of execution before the hypothesis got a fair test)? Most "failed"
   campaigns are actually UNTESTED — say so when true.

2. LAYER AUTOPSY — which funnel layer over/under-performed vs baseline, and what
   that isolates (creative? audience? offer? page? — funnel decomposition logic)

3. DECISIONS vs LUCK — classify the 3-5 biggest decisions:
   good decision/good outcome · good decision/bad outcome (keep making these!) ·
   bad decision/good outcome (the dangerous ones — don't learn the wrong lesson) ·
   bad decision/bad outcome

4. THE RULES EARNED — 0 to 2 transferable rules this campaign actually proved,
   stated with scope ("for cold GCC traffic on this product line…"). If the data
   doesn't earn a rule, output NONE — one campaign is one data point.

5. THE EXPERIMENT LOG ENTRY — 4 lines, ready to append to my log:
   hypothesis → design → result → decision (adopt / kill / retest with change X)

6. NEXT CAMPAIGN INHERITANCE — the ONE thing the next campaign must do
   differently, and the ONE thing it must keep.
```

## 🧠 Pro tips

- Run it while the campaign is still fresh — a postmortem two months later is fan fiction written from dashboards.
- The "good decision / bad outcome" quadrant is the culture-maker: if those get punished, your team stops taking correct risks and starts optimizing for blamelessness.
- Cap rules at two per campaign. Teams that extract five "learnings" per campaign are writing horoscopes.
- Append every entry to one running log file and upload it to your AI advisor's knowledge ([Growth Strategist GPT](../../gpts/growth-strategist-gpt.md)) — it stops the model from re-recommending your graveyard.
- Postmortem your winners with the same rigor — an unexplained win is a loss on a delay.
