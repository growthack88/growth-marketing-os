# Competitor Ads Teardown Prompt

> Turn a competitor's public ad library into a strategy readout: which angles they're betting on, what their funnel architecture looks like from the outside, where their offer is vulnerable — and the counter-positioning move, not just "make similar ads."

## ⚡ What it does

Everyone checks the Meta Ad Library; almost everyone uses it wrong — screenshotting creatives to imitate. But an ad library read properly reveals strategy: longest-running ads = what's actually working; angle distribution = their thesis about the customer; ad-count spikes = testing phases; landing URLs = funnel structure. This prompt extracts that layer and ends where it should: what they've left undefended for you.

## 🎯 When I use it (real scenario)

This is the second step of any market entry or quarterly strategy review I run — after mining our own customers' language, read what competitors are paying to say. The discipline the prompt enforces is the one that matters: the goal is never to copy the winner's ads (you'd be testing their hypothesis with less data), it's to map the angles nobody in the market is running — which is where the [Ad Angle Matrix](ad-angle-matrix.md) picks up.

## 📋 The Prompt

```
You are a competitive strategist for paid social. I'm pasting ads collected from a competitor's public ad library. Read the STRATEGY, not just the creatives.

MY CONTEXT:
- My product & positioning: [X]
- The competitor: [name + what they sell]
- Market: [X]

THEIR ADS (for each: ad copy, creative description, start date / "running since", landing URL if visible):
[PASTE ADS — the Meta Ad Library shows launch dates; include them, they matter most]

ANALYZE:

1. LONGEVITY SIGNAL — ads running 60+ days are almost certainly profitable; recent
   batches are tests. Separate the two piles FIRST. The old pile is their real strategy;
   the new pile is their anxiety.

2. ANGLE MAP — classify every ad by driver (pain / dream / mechanism / proof /
   objection / identity / urgency). Output the distribution: what % of their spend-time
   sits on each angle? What does that say about who they think their customer is?

3. FUNNEL ARCHAEOLOGY — from the landing URLs and CTAs: direct-to-product vs
   advertorial vs quiz vs lead magnet? Retargeting tells (DPA formats, testimonial-heavy
   ads)? Sketch their likely funnel.

4. OFFER FORENSICS — price points, discounts, bundles, guarantees visible in the ads.
   What's their offer's strongest claim, and what does it structurally rely on
   (price? speed? status?)?

5. THE GAPS — the deliverable:
   a. UNRUN ANGLES: which of the 7 drivers do they barely touch, and which of those
      fit MY product's real strengths?
   b. UNDEFENDED OBJECTIONS: what customer objection do none of their ads answer?
   c. AUDIENCE BLIND SPOT: who is visibly NOT addressed in their creative choices?

6. COUNTER-POSITIONING PLAY — one paragraph: the angle + offer frame I should lead
   with that exploits the gaps, is true to my product, and does NOT compete on their
   strongest ground. Then 3 hook lines for it.

RULES: infer, but label inference vs observation. Never recommend copying a specific
ad — their winning ad won their hypothesis with their audience data; my job is the
adjacent bet they haven't made.
```

## 🧠 Pro tips

- Collect 20+ ads before running this — five ads reads a mood, twenty reads a strategy. Note "started running" dates for every one; they're the closest thing to competitor revenue data you'll ever get for free.
- Rerun quarterly and diff the angle maps — a competitor shifting angle distribution is telling you what stopped working before their P&L does.
- Teardown 2-3 competitors and stack the gap analyses: an angle missing from ALL of them is either the market's blind spot or a landmine someone already stepped on. Test it small either way.
- Feed the discovered gaps into the [Ad Angle Matrix](ad-angle-matrix.md) as priority angles, and their unanswered objections into your [offer stack](../growth/offer-stack-builder.md) as bonus/guarantee design.
- MENA note: also check dialect register — a market full of فصحى or translated-English ads is leaving the entire "sounds like a human" position open (see the [Bilingual Hook Bank](../../swipe-files/hooks/bilingual-hook-bank.md)).
