# Ad Angle Matrix Prompt

> Generate 12 genuinely distinct ad angles from one product — mapped across awareness levels and psychological drivers — so your next creative batch tests different arguments, not different fonts.

## ⚡ What it does

Most "new creative batches" are the same angle re-skinned: same promise, new b-roll. When it fatigues, the account stalls and everyone blames the algorithm. This prompt forces angle diversity at the strategy layer: it decomposes your product into pains, dreams, mechanisms, objections, and identities, then crosses them with awareness levels to output a 12-angle matrix — each angle a different reason to buy, not a different way to say the same reason.

## 🎯 When I use it (real scenario)

This is my batch-planning step before any creative production sprint — for e-com offers and digital products alike. The pattern that made it a fixed ritual: accounts where every ad hammered the same "best price" angle while the actual buyers, when you read the comments and DMs, were buying for a completely different reason (status, fear of missing the trend, a specific painful moment). The matrix surfaces those alternative arguments systematically instead of waiting for a lucky creative to reveal them.

## 📋 The Prompt

```
You are a direct-response creative strategist. Build me an ad angle testing matrix — 12 DISTINCT angles, not 12 phrasings of one angle.

PRODUCT:
- What it is + price: [X]
- Market & audience: [X]
- Top 3 real reasons customers say they bought (from reviews/DMs/comments): [X]
- Top 3 objections before buying: [X]
- The "enemy": what are they using/doing instead today? [X]

BUILD THE MATRIX — 12 ANGLES ACROSS THESE DRIVERS (at least one each):
1. PAIN — the specific ugly moment the product ends
2. DREAM OUTCOME — life after, shown concretely (not "feel confident" — show the scene)
3. MECHANISM — why this works when everything else failed (the "unique lever")
4. PROOF/RESULT — lead with the receipt
5. OBJECTION FLIP — take the #1 objection and make it the headline
6. IDENTITY — who they become / which tribe they join by buying
7. ENEMY/CONTRAST — against the status quo or the old way (not competitors by name)
8. URGENCY/EVENT — a real reason it matters NOW (season, trend, price change)

FOR EACH OF THE 12 ANGLES OUTPUT:
| # | Awareness level (unaware → most aware) | Driver | The argument in one sentence | Hook line (first 3 seconds) | Suggested format (UGC / static / demo / founder-face) |

THEN:
- Rank the 12 by expected testing priority for MY audience temperature and explain the top 3 picks in one line each
- Flag which angles need proof assets I may not have yet (testimonials, before/after) so I don't script what I can't back up
```

## 🧠 Pro tips

- Feed it REAL customer language (reviews, DMs, support tickets) — the matrix is only as sharp as the voice-of-customer you paste in.
- Test angles with cheap formats first (static + text overlay). Produce expensive video only for angles that survived.
- One angle per ad set. If you mix angles in one creative, you learn nothing when it wins.
- Rerun the matrix quarterly — the winning angle shifts as your audience saturates, and last quarter's #7 becomes this quarter's #1.
