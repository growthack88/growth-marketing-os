# Review Mining Copy Bank Prompt

> Turn raw reviews, DMs, and support messages into a structured voice-of-customer vault — the customer's exact words sorted by desire, objection, trigger, and outcome — so your copy quotes the market instead of guessing at it.

## ⚡ What it does

The best-converting lines in any account are rarely written — they're transcribed. Customers describe the problem, the hesitation, and the payoff in words no copywriter invents ("I ordered it for my sister and kept it"). This prompt processes your raw feedback pile into a reusable bank: phrases classified by funnel job, kept verbatim (typos and dialect intact), each tagged with where to deploy it — hook, headline, objection block, or testimonial pull-quote.

## 🎯 When I use it (real scenario)

This is the prep step behind the customer-language inputs my other prompts demand — the [Ad Angle Matrix](../paid-ads/ad-angle-matrix.md) asks for "real reasons customers say they bought" and this is where those come from. Running it on Arabic feedback is where it earns its keep twice: customer عامية is the register the [CTA Bank](../../swipe-files/ctas/arabic-cta-bank.md) and offer pages need, and mined phrases arrive pre-written in it — no localization pass required, because the customer already did it.

## 📋 The Prompt

```
You are a voice-of-customer researcher. Process my raw customer feedback into a copy bank. PRESERVE EXACT WORDING — typos, dialect, mixed languages and all. Never paraphrase; paraphrasing destroys the asset.

RAW MATERIAL (paste everything — reviews, DMs, WhatsApp messages, support tickets, survey answers, comments):
[PASTE HERE]

CONTEXT:
- Product & market: [X]
- Languages in the data: [EN / AR / mixed]

BUILD THE BANK — classify every usable phrase into:

1. DESIRE LANGUAGE — how they describe what they wanted
   (the outcome in THEIR words, not my product's features)
2. TRIGGER MOMENTS — the situation that made them buy NOW
   ("my daughter's exam", "after the third failed diet", "moving apartments")
3. OBJECTION LANGUAGE — hesitations, stated or implied
   ("thought it was too good to be true", "was worried about shipping")
4. SURPRISE & DELIGHT — what exceeded expectations (these become differentiators)
5. COMPARISON LANGUAGE — what they used before / instead, in their words
6. IDENTITY PHRASES — how they describe themselves
   ("as a working mom...", "I'm not a tech person")

FOR EACH ENTRY:
| Verbatim phrase | Language/register | Funnel deployment (ad hook / headline / objection block / testimonial / CTA microcopy) | Strength (how many times variants of this appeared) |

THEN OUTPUT:
- THE TOP 5: strongest verbatim phrases overall + exactly where I should deploy each
- THE GAP REPORT: which categories are thin — and the 3 questions I should ask
  customers (post-purchase survey / WhatsApp) to fill them
- REGISTER NOTE (if Arabic data): which dialect/register my customers actually
  write in — this is the register my copy should match
```

## 🧠 Pro tips

- Feed it losses too: cancelled orders, refund reasons, "why didn't you buy?" replies — objection language from non-buyers converts better than praise from fans.
- Rerun quarterly and diff the banks: shifting trigger moments are your earliest signal that the market's buying reason moved before your metrics show it.
- The frequency column is the ranking: a phrase three customers used independently beats your best headline. Ship it nearly verbatim.
- Verbatim means verbatim in ads too — cleaning up the grammar of a quote is how it stops sounding like a customer.
- Pipeline: this bank → [Ad Angle Matrix](../paid-ads/ad-angle-matrix.md) inputs → [UGC briefs](../social/ugc-brief-generator.md) hooks. One mining session feeds a quarter of creative work.
