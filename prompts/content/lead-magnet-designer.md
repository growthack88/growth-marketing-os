# Lead Magnet Designer Prompt

> Design a lead magnet backwards from the paid product: it solves one real problem completely, reveals the bigger problem honestly, and makes the paid product the obvious next step — not a surprise pitch.

## ⚡ What it does

Most lead magnets are built forwards ("what freebie can we make?") and collect emails that never convert, because the freebie has no relationship to the sale. This prompt designs backwards: start from the paid product, find the problem slice whose solution naturally exposes the need for the rest, and package that slice as the magnet. The gap between what the magnet solves and what the product solves is engineered, not accidental.

## 🎯 When I use it (real scenario)

This backwards logic comes from building free tools and checklists around my own paid products — where the pattern became obvious: the freebies that converted weren't the most generous ones, they were the ones that ended exactly where the paid product begins. A free readiness checklist converts to a paid toolkit because completing the checklist IS discovering you need the toolkit. Generosity gets thank-yous; engineered gaps get customers.

## 📋 The Prompt

```
You are a funnel strategist. Design a lead magnet BACKWARDS from my paid product.

MY PAID PRODUCT:
- What it is + price: [X]
- The full transformation it delivers: [X]
- Audience + where this traffic comes from: [X]
- The #1 reason people DON'T buy when they first hear of it: [X]

DESIGN PROCESS — show your work at each step:

1. SLICE SELECTION
   List 5 candidate problem-slices of the full transformation. For each: does solving
   it alone create the need for the rest? Score /10 on "gap quality". Pick the winner.

2. THE MAGNET
   - Format matched to consumption time (checklist / template / mini-audit / calculator
     / swipe file / short video) — bias toward "usable in under 15 minutes"
   - Exactly what's inside (outline it fully)
   - The AHA moment: the point where the user gets real value AND sees the bigger problem
   - The bridge line: the sentence at the end that makes the paid product the obvious
     next step (helpful tone, not a pitch pivot)

3. TITLES — 5 options
   Specific outcome + effort collapse ("The 10-minute X that Y"). No clever wordplay
   that hides what it is.

4. DELIVERY EMAIL
   Subject + 80-word body: deliver the asset, set expectation for what emails come next,
   one line planting the paid product without selling it.

5. THE HONESTY CHECK
   Confirm: does the magnet genuinely solve its slice? If it's a teaser with no standalone
   value, redesign it — burned trust at the top of the funnel compounds downward.
```

## 🧠 Pro tips

- One magnet per traffic temperature: cold traffic needs a smaller, faster slice than warm audiences. Don't force one freebie to serve both.
- Templates and checklists beat ebooks: consumption rate is the hidden metric — an unopened PDF converts exactly nothing.
- Put the bridge line INSIDE the asset, not just in the follow-up emails — the moment of value is the moment of maximum attention.
- Interactive magnets (scorecards, calculators, mini-audits) double as segmentation: the user's answers tell you which paid pitch fits them.
