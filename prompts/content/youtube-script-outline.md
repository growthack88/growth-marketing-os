# YouTube Script Outline Prompt (Retention-First)

> Turn a topic into a complete script outline engineered for retention: cold open, promise, open loops, segment payoffs, and an end-screen bridge to the next video — before you write a single full sentence.

## ⚡ What it does

Most educational scripts are written like blog posts: intro → points → outro. YouTube punishes that structure — the intro alone can lose a third of viewers before the content starts. This prompt outlines the video the way retention graphs actually reward: payoff visible in the first 30 seconds, loops that stay open across segments, and the biggest insight placed where drop-off usually spikes, not saved politely for the end.

## 🎯 When I use it (real scenario)

This structure comes from scripting for my own marketing education channel, where the same lesson kept repeating: the videos that held retention weren't the ones with better information — they were the ones where the structure kept re-earning attention every 60-90 seconds. The outline stage is where that's decided; by the time you're writing full sentences it's too late to fix the architecture. Works in Arabic exactly as in English — the retention mechanics don't care about the language.

## 📋 The Prompt

```
You are a YouTube script strategist for educational/business channels. Build me a retention-first script OUTLINE — structure and beats, not full sentences yet.

INPUT:
- Video topic & the ONE core insight: [X]
- Audience & what they already know: [X]
- Target length: [X] minutes
- My proof/examples available for this topic: [list — screenshots, stories, data, demos]
- Language: [EN / Arabic عامية / both]
- The next video I want them to watch after this: [X]

BUILD THE OUTLINE:

1. COLD OPEN (0:00-0:15) — 3 alternative openings, each a different type:
   a) result-first (show the payoff exists)
   b) contrarian claim
   c) mid-action start (begin inside the most interesting moment)
   NO "hey guys, welcome back". NO channel intro.

2. PROMISE STACK (0:15-0:45) — what exactly they'll be able to DO by the end + why it matters now + one credibility line (use my proof list, don't invent).

3. SEGMENTS (the body) — for each segment:
   - The beat: what's taught
   - The open loop: what question this segment deliberately leaves hanging for the next one
   - The visual/proof moment: what's on screen beside my face
   - Approx timestamp
   Place the STRONGEST insight at the 40-60% mark — where educational videos bleed viewers — not at the end.

4. PAYOFF — the moment the promise is fully delivered. Mark it explicitly.

5. END-SCREEN BRIDGE (last 20s) — no "like & subscribe" begging. One sentence that makes [next video] the obvious continuation of the thought they're already having.

THEN OUTPUT:
- The retention risk map: the 2 spots in THIS outline where people will most likely leave, and the fix for each
- Title options (5) + thumbnail concept (1 line each) that match the cold open — title, thumbnail, and open must tell ONE story
```

## 🧠 Pro tips

- Outline first, always. A great script on a broken structure still gets 25% retention.
- The open-loop column is the whole game — if a segment doesn't leave a question hanging, viewers have permission to leave.
- For Arabic channels: keep the cold open in عامية even if the technical content code-switches — the first 15 seconds must sound like a person, not a lecture.
- Steal your cold opens from your own analytics: whichever past video has the best 30-second retention, that opening type is your audience's preference.
- Title + thumbnail BEFORE final script: if you can't package it, re-angle the video now, not after editing.
