# Abandoned Cart Recovery Sequence Prompt (COD & MENA-Ready)

> Generate a complete 3-touch cart recovery sequence — email or WhatsApp — that handles the real abandonment reasons in COD markets: shipping cost shock, payment trust, and "I'll order later" inertia.

## ⚡ What it does

Every cart recovery template on the internet assumes a US buyer with a saved credit card. In Egypt, GCC, and most COD-heavy markets the cart dies at different moments: the shipping fee reveal, the "do I trust this store with my phone number" hesitation, and the classic order-later-forget-forever. This prompt generates a 3-touch sequence engineered around those exact objections, with native عامية Arabic output that sounds like a person, not a translation plugin.

## 🎯 When I use it (real scenario)

Built from running an Arabic-language Shopify book store where cash-on-delivery is the default and the shipping-fee step is where carts go to die. The sequence structure came from watching where orders actually stalled: touch 1 assumes they got distracted, touch 2 kills the shipping/trust objection head-on, touch 3 gives a clean reason to act today. Same skeleton works for email and WhatsApp — in COD markets, WhatsApp recovery outperforms email so consistently it should be your first channel, not your backup.

## 📋 The Prompt

```
You are a retention copywriter specialized in COD and MENA e-commerce. Write my abandoned cart recovery sequence.

STORE CONTEXT:
- Product & price range: [X]
- Market & language: [Egypt عامية / GCC / bilingual EN+AR]
- Payment methods: [COD / card / wallet] — COD share if known: [X]%
- Shipping: [fee, free-shipping threshold, delivery time]
- Channel for this sequence: [email / WhatsApp / both]
- Brand voice: [friendly / premium / playful]
- Incentive I'm willing to give (if any): [discount %, free shipping, gift — or "none"]

WRITE 3 TOUCHES:

TOUCH 1 — "You forgot something" (send: 1-3 hours after abandonment)
- Assume distraction, not objection. Zero pressure, zero discount.
- Show the exact product left behind. One CTA back to cart.

TOUCH 2 — OBJECTION KILLER (send: ~24 hours)
- Attack the top COD-market objections directly: shipping cost, delivery time, return policy, "is this store real" trust.
- Include social proof placeholder + COD reassurance line ("pay when you receive it").
- No discount yet.

TOUCH 3 — REASON TO ACT NOW (send: ~48-72 hours)
- Give the incentive (if provided) OR an honest urgency angle (stock, price validity, delivery-by date).
- No fake countdown timers. If there is no real urgency, use loss framing on the incentive instead.

RULES:
- Arabic output must be Egyptian عامية with natural English code-switching for business terms (checkout, order, offer) — NEVER فصحى.
- WhatsApp versions: max 3 short lines + CTA link. Emails: subject + preview text + body under 120 words.
- Every touch needs: subject/opening line, body, CTA button text.
- Output each touch in [language(s) requested], clearly labeled.
```

## 🧠 Pro tips

- Don't lead with a discount in touch 1 — you'll train customers to abandon carts on purpose. Save it for touch 3, if at all.
- In COD markets, put "الدفع عند الاستلام" (pay on delivery) visibly in touch 2 — it's a conversion element, not a footnote.
- WhatsApp recovery needs opt-in and a human sender name. A message from "أحمد من المتجر" beats a message from the store name.
- If your abandonment spikes at the shipping step (check your checkout funnel), fix the shipping-fee surprise first — no sequence out-writes a broken offer.
