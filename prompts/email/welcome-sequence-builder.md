# Welcome Sequence Builder Prompt

> Generate a 5-email welcome sequence built around one spine: the single belief a subscriber must change to become a buyer. Every email either delivers value or moves that belief — no filler "about us" episodes.

## ⚡ What it does

Welcome sequences fail two ways: the info-dump (five emails about the brand nobody asked about) and the instant-pitch (discount in email one, unsubscribes by email three). This prompt builds the sequence around the belief gap instead — it makes you name the ONE thing subscribers currently believe that stops them buying, then engineers each email as a step across that gap: deliver → shift → prove → de-risk → offer.

## 🎯 When I use it (real scenario)

Distilled from building lifecycle email flows for my own products — trial nurtures, launch warmups, lead-magnet follow-ups. The belief-spine idea came from watching openers who never bought: they liked the content fine; they just never changed their mind about the thing that mattered ("I can do this manually", "this is for bigger businesses than mine"). Once each sequence got one named belief to move, the emails stopped being a content schedule and became an argument.

## 📋 The Prompt

```
You are a lifecycle email strategist. Build my 5-email welcome sequence around ONE belief shift.

CONTEXT:
- How they joined the list: [lead magnet / purchase / trial signup / content subscribe]
- What I ultimately want them to buy: [product + price]
- THE BELIEF GAP: what they believe now that stops them buying: [X]
  → what they must believe instead: [X]
  (If I left this blank: infer the 3 most likely belief gaps from the product and audience, pick the strongest, and state your choice.)
- Voice: [me/founder personal · brand] — Language: [EN / Egyptian عامية / both]
- Real proof assets I have: [testimonials, results, screenshots, story — list honestly]

BUILD 5 EMAILS (name each email's JOB, then write it):

EMAIL 1 — DELIVER + FRAME (send immediately)
Deliver what they came for. One line that frames the journey ("over the next few days
I'll show you X"). NO pitch. End with an open loop for email 2.

EMAIL 2 — THE BELIEF SHIFT (day 1-2)
The argument email. Attack the old belief with a story or a reframe — not with claims.
This is the sequence's centerpiece; write it as the founder talking, not the brand broadcasting.

EMAIL 3 — PROOF (day 3)
Evidence for the new belief, using ONLY the proof assets I listed. One proof deep,
not five proofs shallow. End with "what this means for you".

EMAIL 4 — OBJECTION + DE-RISK (day 4-5)
Name the #1 remaining objection out loud and answer it honestly (including who this
is NOT for — exclusion builds trust). Introduce the risk reversal.

EMAIL 5 — THE OFFER (day 5-7)
Now sell, cleanly: what it is, who it's for, stack summary, one honest urgency angle
if one exists. Single CTA repeated twice.

PER EMAIL OUTPUT: subject + preview text (they must pair, not repeat) · body under
150 words · CTA · the belief-move this email makes.

RULES: عامية with English business terms if Arabic — never فصحى. No fake countdown.
Write like one person emailing one person.
```

## 🧠 Pro tips

- If you can't name the belief gap, that's the real problem — survey five customers ("what almost stopped you from buying?") before writing a single email.
- Email 2 is where sequences live or die. Spend half your total effort there.
- Keep email 1's deliverability job in mind: ask a question worth replying to — replies train inbox providers that you're wanted mail.
- Segment by entry point when volume justifies it: a lead-magnet subscriber and a trial signup have different belief gaps; one sequence can't argue both.
