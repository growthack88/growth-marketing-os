# Arabic CTA Bank (EN + AR)

> 20 call-to-action lines for buttons, ads, and closings — organized by intent stage, each with an English version, a native عامية Arabic version, and why it works. Built for MENA e-commerce and digital products.

## ⚡ What it does

The CTA is the last 3 words before the money, and in Arabic it's where most stores fall apart: formal فصحى buttons («اشترِ الآن», «سجّل الآن») that no human says out loud. This bank gives you stage-matched CTAs — cold click, warm cart, COD checkout, trial, lead magnet — in the spoken Arabic that matches how your customers actually talk.

## 🎯 When I use it (real scenario)

Collected from building Arabic RTL offer pages for digital products and an Arabic Shopify book store, where every page's buy button deep-links straight into checkout — meaning the CTA copy carries the entire final step alone, with no cart page to soften it. The stage-matching rule came from that work: a «اطلب دلوقتي» (order now) button converts on a warm offer page but reads as pushy on a cold blog visitor, who needs «شوف التفاصيل» (see the details) first.

## 📋 The Bank

**PURCHASE — warm traffic, offer page (high intent)**
1. EN: "Get instant access" · AR: «ابدأ فورًا — التسليم لحظي»
   *Why: "instant" kills the delivery-anxiety objection for digital products.*
2. EN: "Order now — pay on delivery" · AR: «اطلب دلوقتي وادفع عند الاستلام»
   *Why: the strongest CTA in COD markets — it IS the risk-reversal.*
3. EN: "Yes, I want this" · AR: «آه، أنا عايز ده»
   *Why: first-person affirmation; reads as the customer's own voice, not the store's.*
4. EN: "Claim your copy" · AR: «احجز نسختك»
   *Why: ownership framing — the copy is already "yours", you're just claiming it.*

**COD & CHECKOUT REASSURANCE — the button's supporting line**
5. EN: "Pay when it arrives — no card needed" · AR: «الدفع لما الأوردر يوصلك — من غير فيزا»
6. EN: "Free returns within 14 days" · AR: «استرجاع مجاني خلال 14 يوم»
7. EN: "Delivery in 2-4 days" · AR: «التوصيل خلال 2-4 أيام»
   *Why: these three lines under the button answer the three questions every COD buyer asks before tapping.*

**TRIAL / FREEMIUM — SaaS & apps**
8. EN: "Start free — no card required" · AR: «جرّب ببلاش — من غير كارت»
9. EN: "See it on your own data" · AR: «جرّبه على شغلك انت»
   *Why: "your own data/work" beats generic "try now" — it previews the aha moment.*
10. EN: "Takes 2 minutes to set up" · AR: «الإعداد بياخد دقيقتين»
    *Why: effort collapse next to the button, not in the FAQ.*

**LEAD MAGNET / CONTENT**
11. EN: "Send me the checklist" · AR: «ابعتولي الـ checklist»
    *Why: imperative from the reader's side — they're commanding you, not submitting a form.*
12. EN: "Get the free template" · AR: «خد الـ template ببلاش»
13. EN: "Read the full breakdown" · AR: «اقرا التفاصيل كاملة»
    *Why: for cold traffic — asks for attention, not commitment.*

**URGENCY CLOSERS — only when the scarcity is real**
14. EN: "Founding price ends [date]" · AR: «سعر التأسيس لحد [التاريخ] بس»
15. EN: "[X] left at this price" · AR: «فاضل [X] بالسعر ده»
16. EN: "Price goes up next batch" · AR: «الدفعة الجاية السعر هيزيد»
    *Why: batch/cohort framing makes the price rise a fact of the calendar, not a threat.*

**SOFT / RELATIONSHIP — email closings & retargeting**
17. EN: "Your cart is waiting" · AR: «الأوردر بتاعك لسه مستنيك»
18. EN: "Questions? Just reply" · AR: «لو عندك سؤال، رد على الرسالة دي وهرد عليك»
    *Why: a human promise; doubles as deliverability signal when people actually reply.*
19. EN: "Come back when you're ready" · AR: «خد وقتك — واحنا موجودين»
    *Why: zero-pressure retargeting line that earns the next click by not begging for this one.*
20. EN: "Was this useful? Share it" · AR: «لو استفدت، ابعتها لحد محتاجها»
    *Why: the Arabic version frames sharing as helping a friend — the strongest share trigger in MENA audiences.*

## 🧠 Pro tips

- Match CTA temperature to traffic temperature — the fastest CRO win on Arabic pages is swapping a hot CTA off a cold page.
- Never mix فصحى and عامية on the same page. Pick the register and hold it everywhere: button, form labels, error messages.
- On RTL pages, test button copy length on mobile first — Arabic runs ~20% longer and wraps ugly on small screens.
- The line UNDER the button (reassurance micro-copy, #5-7) often moves conversion more than the button itself. Don't leave it empty on COD offers.
