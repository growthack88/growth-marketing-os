# Bilingual Subject Line Bank (EN + AR)

> 24 subject line formulas organized by trigger — each with an English version, a native عامية Arabic version, and the situation it fits. The Arabic versions are written, not translated.

## ⚡ What it does

Subject lines decide whether the rest of your email exists. Arabic email marketing has a special problem: most brands write subject lines in فصحى that reads like a bank notification — and bank notifications get ignored professionally. This bank gives you human-register openers in both languages, organized by trigger so you match the line to the email's actual job, plus the preview-text pairing rule most senders waste.

## 🎯 When I use it (real scenario)

Collected from writing launch and lifecycle emails for my own products and lists — bilingual audiences where the same announcement goes out in both languages and the Arabic version has to sound like me, not like my translator. The recurring lesson baked into these: specificity beats cleverness in the inbox, and in Arabic, adding one spoken-register word («بصراحة», «معلش», «خد بالك») does more for opens than any emoji.

## 📋 The Bank

**CURIOSITY GAP — daily/newsletter emails**
1. EN: "The mistake in your [thing] you can't see" · AR: «الغلطة اللي في الـ [thing] بتاعك ومش شايفها»
2. EN: "Nobody talks about this stage" · AR: «المرحلة دي محدش بيتكلم عنها»
3. EN: "I was wrong about [topic]" · AR: «كنت غلطان في موضوع الـ [topic]»
   *Why: admission > authority for open rates — it promises a real update, not a lecture.*

**SPECIFICITY / NUMBERS — value & teaching emails**
4. EN: "3 lines that fix your checkout" · AR: «3 سطور تصلّح الـ checkout عندك»
5. EN: "The 5-minute audit before you spend" · AR: «Audit في 5 دقايق قبل ما تدفع مليم في إعلانات»
6. EN: "One metric. Check it weekly." · AR: «رقم واحد بس. بصّ عليه كل أسبوع.»

**LOSS / MISTAKE — re-engagement & warning emails**
7. EN: "You're paying the [X] tax and don't know it" · AR: «انت دافع ضريبة الـ [X] من غير ما تحس»
8. EN: "Delete this before your next campaign" · AR: «امسح ده قبل الحملة الجاية»
9. EN: "Where the money actually leaks" · AR: «الفلوس بتهرب منين بالظبط»

**PERSONAL / STORY — relationship & launch-warmup emails**
10. EN: "I almost shut it down" · AR: «كنت هقفّل المشروع»
11. EN: "What I'd do with $500 and zero audience" · AR: «لو معايا 500 دولار ومفيش audience — هعمل ايه؟»
12. EN: "The client call that changed my pricing" · AR: «مكالمة العميل اللي غيّرت التسعير عندي»

**LAUNCH / OFFER — announcement emails**
13. EN: "It's live: [offer name]" · AR: «نزل رسميًا: [اسم العرض]»
14. EN: "Founding price ends Thursday" · AR: «سعر التأسيس لحد الخميس بس»
15. EN: "[Name], the bundle you asked about" · AR: «يا [الاسم]، الـ bundle اللي سألت عليه»
    *Why: works only if they DID ask (segment it) — fake familiarity burns trust permanently.*

**QUESTION — engagement & survey emails**
16. EN: "Quick one: which of these are you?" · AR: «سؤال سريع: انت مين فيهم؟»
17. EN: "What's blocking your launch?" · AR: «ايه اللي موقّف الـ launch بتاعك؟»

**ABANDONMENT / TRANSACTIONAL-ADJACENT**
18. EN: "Your cart's still here (no rush)" · AR: «الأوردر لسه محفوظ ليك (من غير استعجال)»
19. EN: "About your order — one thing" · AR: «بخصوص الأوردر بتاعك — حاجة واحدة بس»
    *Why: near-transactional framing gets near-transactional opens; use honestly or not at all.*

**RE-PERMISSION / WIN-BACK**
20. EN: "Should I stop emailing you?" · AR: «أبطّل أبعتلك إيميلات؟»
21. EN: "Still into [topic]?" · AR: «لسه مهتم بالـ [topic]؟»

**THE ANTI-FORMULAS — use sparingly, they spike then fatigue**
22. EN: "hey" · AR: «عامل ايه»
23. EN: "(don't open this)" · AR: «(متفتحش الإيميل ده)»
24. EN: "Re: your question" · AR: «رد: سؤالك»
    *Why: #24 only when there IS a thread/question — as a cold trick it's a spam-report magnet.*

**THE PREVIEW-TEXT RULE:** the preview line must ADD information, never repeat the subject. Subject = curiosity, preview = stakes: "The 5-minute audit before you spend" + "It found the leak in campaign #3." Treat them as one two-line hook.

## 🧠 Pro tips

- Test trigger vs trigger (curiosity vs specificity), not wording vs wording — that's how you learn what your list responds to, not which synonym won.
- In Arabic, the register IS the differentiator: an inbox full of فصحى notifications makes one عامية line feel like a message from a friend. That's the whole edge.
- Keep a kill-list of your own fatigued formulas — any formula that wins gets copied by your own future emails until it stops working. Rotate quarterly.
- Under 40 characters survives mobile truncation in both languages — Arabic counts fewer words but similar characters; check, don't assume.
- Pair with the [Abandoned Cart Recovery Sequence](../../prompts/email/abandoned-cart-recovery-sequence.md) — touches 1-3 map to formulas #18, #19, and #14.
