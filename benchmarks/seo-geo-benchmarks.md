# SEO & GEO Benchmarks (2026, Sourced)

> Reference data for search strategy in the AI era: organic CTR by position, the measured impact of AI Overviews, zero-click share, LLM referral traffic reality, what generative engines cite, and the Arabic content gap — all cited, all dated, because these numbers move monthly.

## ⚡ What it does

Search benchmarks aged faster in the last two years than in the previous ten: AI Overviews rewired CTR curves, zero-click crossed two-thirds, and a new discipline (GEO) got its first real studies. This file collects the current measured numbers — not the LinkedIn-post versions of them — with the methodology conflicts shown, because in this domain the disagreements ARE the information.

## 🎯 When I use it (real scenario)

This data is the "why" layer under my GEO work — the [llms.txt](../prompts/seo/llms-txt-generator.md) and [entity schema](../prompts/seo/entity-schema-builder.md) assets, and the entity-hub build on my own site. The two findings that shaped that strategy appear below with sources: being cited inside an AI answer lifts CTR (+35% organic per Seer) while merely ranking under one loses it, and the Arabic web is ~10x under-represented relative to its users — meaning structured Arabic content gets cited by AI engines with almost no competition.

## 📋 The Benchmarks

### 1 · Organic CTR by position (quote the range, not a point)

| Position | FirstPageSage 2025 | Backlinko (4M results) |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 39.8% | 27.6% |
| 2 | 18.7% | 15.8% |
| 3 | 10.2% | 11.0% |
| 5 | 5.1% | ~7% |
| 10 | 1.6% | — |

Top-3 capture ~54% of all clicks. The ~12-point spread at position 1 is methodology (panel mix); treat position-1 CTR as **20-40%**, context-dependent. [FirstPageSage](https://firstpagesage.com/reports/google-click-through-rates-ctrs-by-ranking-position/) · [Backlinko](https://backlinko.com/google-ctr-stats)

### 2 · AI Overviews — the measured click impact

| Finding | Number | Study |
|---|---|---|
| AIO present → #1 result CTR | −34.5% (Apr 2025) → **−58%** (Dec 2025 update) | [Ahrefs, 300K keywords](https://ahrefs.com/blog/ai-overviews-reduce-clicks/) · [update](https://ahrefs.com/blog/ai-overviews-reduce-clicks-update/) |
| Informational queries with AIO, organic CTR | 1.76% → 0.61% (−61%) | [Seer Interactive, 25M impressions, 2025](https://www.seerinteractive.com/insights/aio-impact-on-google-ctr-september-2025-update) |
| Paid CTR on the same queries | −68% | same |
| **Being CITED in the AIO** | **+35% organic CTR, +91% paid** vs uncited | same |
| Avg CTR change when a keyword gains an AIO | −15.5% (non-branded −20%; branded +18.7%) | [Amsive, 700K keywords, 2025](https://www.amsive.com/insights/seo/google-ai-overviews-new-research-reveals-how-to-navigate-click-drop-off/) |
| Share of queries showing an AIO | 6.5% (Jan 2025) → ~25% (Jul) → ~16% (Nov 2025) — volatile | [Semrush, 10M+ keywords](https://www.semrush.com/blog/semrush-ai-overviews-study/) |
| AIO intent mix | informational share fell 91% → 57% as transactional AIOs grew 2% → 14% (2025) | same |

The strategic read: the penalty for ranking under an AI answer is large and growing; the reward for being the answer's source flipped sign. GEO isn't optional insurance anymore — it's the CTR strategy.

### 3 · Zero-click search

Per 1,000 US Google searches, only ~374 clicks reached the open web (2024); zero-click share hit **68% by early 2026** (up from ~60% in 2024, ~45% in 2016). [SparkToro × Datos, 2024](https://sparktoro.com/blog/2024-zero-click-search-study-for-every-1000-us-google-searches-only-374-clicks-go-to-the-open-web-in-the-eu-its-360/) · [SparkToro × Similarweb, 2026](https://sparktoro.com/blog/in-2026-less-than-one-third-of-google-searches-still-send-a-click/) ⚠️ panel switched between studies — trend robust, exact delta isn't.

### 4 · AI search adoption & LLM referrals (the honest picture)

| Finding | Number | Source |
|---|---|---|
| ChatGPT weekly active users | 800M (Oct 2025, announced) | [OpenAI via TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/10/06/sam-altman-says-chatgpt-has-hit-800m-weekly-active-users/) |
| Google search share | below 90% for first time since 2015 (late 2024) | [Statcounter via SEL](https://searchengineland.com/google-search-market-share-drops-2024-450497) |
| AI referrals to top sites | 1.13B visits in one month, +357% YoY (Jun 2025); ChatGPT >80% of them | [Similarweb via TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/07/25/ai-referrals-to-top-websites-were-up-357-year-over-year-in-june-reaching-1-13b/) |
| LLM referrals as share of total sessions | still **<1-2%** (organic ~32% for comparison) | [SEL 13-month GA4 study, 2026](https://searchengineland.com/what-13-months-of-data-reveals-about-llm-traffic-growth-and-conversions-470115) |
| LLM referral conversion | disputed: ~18% in one GA4 portfolio vs worse-than-organic in a Similarweb-based study; ~7% on transactional sites per Similarweb | same · [SEL](https://searchengineland.com/llms-google-referral-conversion-study-463747) · [Similarweb](https://www.similarweb.com/blog/marketing/geo/gen-ai-stats/) |
| ChatGPT users also using Google same month | 95.3% — complement, not substitute (so far) | Similarweb, 2025 |

Honest framing for planning: LLM traffic is tiny in volume, high in intent, and compounding fast — a position-building game, not a this-quarter channel.

### 5 · GEO — what generative engines actually cite

| Finding | Number | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Adding **statistics, quotations, cited sources** to content | up to +40% generative-engine visibility; fluency +15-30%; keyword stuffing performed WORSE than baseline | [Princeton et al., "GEO" paper, KDD 2024, 10K queries](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2311.09735) |
| Most-cited domains | ChatGPT: Reddit/Wikipedia/Medium/Forbes/LinkedIn · Google AI Mode: LinkedIn (~15%)/YouTube/Reddit · Perplexity: Reddit/LinkedIn/NIH | [Semrush, 230K prompts / 100M+ citations, 2025](https://www.semrush.com/blog/most-cited-domains-ai/) |
| Citation volatility | Reddit fell ~60% → ~10% of ChatGPT citation frequency in ~6 weeks | same — any "most-cited" list has a shelf life of months |
| Branded-query citations | 57% go to third-party reviews/social proof; product pages only 12%; thought leadership 5.4% | [Omniscient Digital, 23K sources, 2026](https://beomniscient.com/blog/content-types-cited-in-llms/) |
| Freshness | recently-updated content disproportionately cited in AI search; date-only changes show no benefit | [Ahrefs fresh-content analysis, 2025](https://ahrefs.com/blog/fresh-content/) |
| "The Great Decoupling" | impressions rising while clicks fall across GSC datasets | [Ahrefs, 2025](https://ahrefs.com/blog/the-great-decoupling/) |

The Princeton findings are this repo's own content rules in study form: statistics with sources, quotable structure, no stuffing.

### 6 · The Arabic gap (the opportunity hiding in this file)

Arabic is **~0.6% of website content** ([W3Techs, 2026](https://w3techs.com/technologies/details/cl-ar-/all/all)) versus **~5.2% of global internet users** ([via Wikipedia/InternetWorldStats](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Languages_used_on_the_Internet)) — a ~9-10x under-representation. ⚠️ The widely-quoted "Arabic is 3% of digital content" has no traceable primary source; use the 0.6%-vs-5.2% pairing. Combined with the citation dynamics above: quality structured Arabic content competes for AI citations in a nearly empty field. That's the quantified case behind the [Bilingual Content System](../frameworks/bilingual-content-system.md)'s Tier-2 strategy.

### 7 · Dated-but-canonical (use with the caveat attached)

- First-page results average 1,447 words ([Backlinko, 11.8M results, 2020](https://backlinko.com/search-engine-ranking)) — correlation only, pre-AIO era
- Featured snippets appear on 12.3% of queries, take 8.6% of clicks ([Ahrefs](https://ahrefs.com/blog/featured-snippets-study/)) — and are being displaced by AIOs (−64% snippet presence alongside AIO growth, [Ahrefs 2025](https://ahrefs.com/blog/ai-overview-growth/))

## 🧠 How to use benchmarks

- **Stamp everything "as of [date]".** Ahrefs' own AIO number moved −34.5% → −58% in eight months. A search benchmark without a date is misinformation with confidence.
- **Optimize for citation, not just position**: the only CTR number that IMPROVED with AI Overviews is the cited source's. The [GEO audit](../prompts/seo/geo-llm-visibility-audit.md) + [llms.txt](../prompts/seo/llms-txt-generator.md) + [entity schema](../prompts/seo/entity-schema-builder.md) trio is the implementation.
- **Watch your own decoupling**: rising impressions with falling clicks in GSC isn't your content failing — it's the SERP changing. Measure brand-query growth and AI citations alongside clicks.
- **Third-party proof beats your own pages** for branded AI queries (57% vs 12%) — reviews, listicles, and forum presence are now a GEO channel, not just social proof.
