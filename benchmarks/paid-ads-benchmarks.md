# Paid Ads Benchmarks (2026, Sourced)

> Reference benchmarks for Meta, Google, and TikTok ads — overall and by industry — plus MENA market data. Every figure is cited to a named study with its sample and year; weak vendor-blog numbers are flagged, and gaps are admitted instead of filled with folklore.

## ⚡ What it does

Paid ads benchmarks are the most abused numbers in marketing: lead-gen CVRs quoted at e-commerce stores, US CPMs quoted at Egyptian accounts, 2021 numbers quoted in 2026. This file gives you the current sourced medians with their context attached — which dataset, which objective, which market — so "is this normal?" gets answered against the right panel.

## 🎯 When I use it (real scenario)

These numbers enter my audits at the triage stage: before diagnosing an account (with the [Meta Ads Creative Diagnosis](../prompts/paid-ads/meta-ads-creative-diagnosis.md) or [Search Terms Audit](../prompts/paid-ads/google-ads-search-terms-audit.md)), I check which metrics are actually abnormal for the account's market and model. The most common finding isn't a broken metric — it's a healthy metric being panicked over because someone compared it to the wrong benchmark. Panel-matching first, panic second.

## 📋 The Benchmarks

### 1 · Meta Ads — overall

**E-commerce (Triple Whale — ~35,000 e-com brands, full-year 2025 medians).** [Source](https://www.triplewhale.com/blog/facebook-ads-benchmarks)

| Metric | 2025 | 2024 | Trend |
|---|---|---|---|
| CPM | $14.19 | $11.82 | +20% |
| CTR | 2.19% | 1.93% | +13.5% |
| CVR (purchase) | 1.60% | 1.48% | +8.3% |
| CPA | $38.19 | $37.80 | +1% |
| ROAS | 1.86 | 1.84 | +1.3% |

**Lead-gen / SMB (WordStream/LocaliQ 2025 — US campaigns, medians).** [Source](https://www.wordstream.com/blog/facebook-ads-benchmarks-2025)

| Metric | Traffic campaigns | Leads campaigns |
|---|---|---|
| CTR | 1.71% | 2.59% |
| CPC | $0.70 | $1.92 |
| CVR | — | 7.72% (lead-form) |
| CPL | — | $27.66 (+21% YoY) |

⚠️ Never compare the two CVRs: 1.6% is e-commerce **purchase** conversion; 7.72% is **lead-form** conversion.

Meta e-com CPA by vertical (Triple Whale 2025): Fashion $36.76 · Beauty $37.92 · Food & Bev $38.15 · Health $38.55 · Home & Garden $46.46. ROAS ranges roughly 1.2x (media/publishing) to 2.5x (automotive) by vertical ([rule1 compilation of Triple Whale data](https://rule1.ai/articles/roas-benchmarks)).

### 2 · Google Ads — search

**WordStream/LocaliQ 2026 report (13,474 US search campaigns, Apr 2025–Mar 2026, medians).** [Source](https://www.wordstream.com/blog/2026-google-ads-benchmarks)

| Metric | 2026 | 2025 |
|---|---|---|
| CTR | 6.64% | 6.66% |
| CPC | $5.42 | $5.26 |
| CVR | 8.18% | 7.52% |
| CPL | $66.69 (first decline in 5 years) | $70.11 |

By industry (2026): Finance & Insurance CTR 9.83% / CPC $3.39 / CVR 2.64% · Education CVR 13.14% · Health & Fitness CPC $6.17 · Shopping/Gifts CPL $49.40 (lowest of majors). Extremes from the 2025 set: Attorneys CPC $8.58 high, Arts & Entertainment $1.60 low.

**Performance Max (Optmyzr, 24,702-campaign study, 2024-25).** [Source](https://www.optmyzr.com/blog/performance-max-2025-updates-study-analysis/)
- PMax CPCs run ~50% cheaper than Search (visual inventory mix)
- PMax at **10–25% of account budget** showed the best CVR/CPA balance
- Multiple PMax campaigns with a single asset group each = best ROAS/CPA structure

⚠️ Google **Display** benchmarks (the oft-quoted 0.46% CTR / $0.63 CPC): recycled pre-2020 data — no fresh large-sample public display study exists. Treat as unknown, not as those numbers.

| CTR | 1.77% | 1.56% |
| CVR | 2.01% | 2.15% |
| CPA | $32.74 | $30.14 |
| ROAS | 2.21 | 2.34 |

[Source](https://www.triplewhale.com/blog/tiktok-benchmarks). ⚠️ Vendor datasets disagree on TikTok (Lebesgue reports 0.61% avg CTR, ~$4.80 CPM — different objective mix); DTC medians from [Varos](https://varos.com/benchmarks/tiktok-cpc) put TikTok CPC at $0.50 vs Facebook $1.72 vs Instagram $3.56. Quote ranges, not points.

| Meta CPM by market (e-com) | US $16.08 · Qatar $12.67 · KSA $12.01 · UAE $10.00 · Kuwait $8.07 · **Egypt $1.81** | [Lebesgue country CPM data, 2026](https://lebesgue.io/facebook-ads/facebook-cpm-by-country) ⚠️ methodology undisclosed — directional |

The Egypt CPM gap is the strategic headline: reach in Egypt costs ~1/9th of US CPMs — creative testing there is cheap; margins and AOV are the constraint, not media cost. **No reliable public MENA Google Ads or CPC benchmarks exist** — anyone quoting exact MENA CPCs is quoting their own account.

### 5 · Creative & fatigue thresholds (practitioner heuristics — flagged as such)

- Hook rate (3-sec views ÷ impressions, Meta): <15% weak · 15-25% average · 25-35% strong · 40%+ elite ([Motion](https://motionapp.com/blog/key-creative-performance-metrics), 2025-26 — practitioner consensus, no disclosed sample)
- Hold rate (ThruPlay ÷ 3-sec views): 40-50% average, >60% strong ([AdSights](https://www.adsights.ai/resources/glossary/metrics/hold-rate))
- The famous "Meta says CTR drops 41% after frequency 4" claim: **not traceable to any primary Meta publication** — use your own frequency-vs-CTR curve instead
- Independent UGC-vs-studio study: none exists; the circulating "+38% CTR" figures are vendor marketing

## 🧠 How to use benchmarks (rules that survive every platform change)

- **Match the panel before the number**: Triple Whale = Shopify DTC e-com; LocaliQ = US SMB lead-gen. Comparing your account to the wrong panel produces confident wrong conclusions.
- **Medians, not averages** — one viral outlier drags averages; every table above is medians where the source offers them.
- **Trends > levels**: CPMs rising ~20%/year (Meta e-com) means flat ROAS = improving efficiency. Judge your account against its own baseline first, the benchmark second.
- **Your account IS a dataset**: after ~90 days of spend, your own layer-by-layer baselines beat any public benchmark — use these tables to calibrate targets, then graduate to your own numbers via the [Campaign Postmortem](../prompts/paid-ads/campaign-postmortem.md) log.
