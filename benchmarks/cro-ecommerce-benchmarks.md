# CRO & E-commerce Benchmarks (2026, Sourced)

> Reference benchmarks for the full e-commerce funnel — sitewide conversion by panel, stage-by-stage rates, abandonment reasons, AOV, and COD-market data for MENA — with every figure cited and panel bias explained.

## ⚡ What it does

"What's a good conversion rate?" has no answer without three qualifiers: which panel (Shopify SMBs convert at a 1.4% median while enterprise panels read ~3%), which stage, and which market. This file organizes the current sourced numbers by exactly those qualifiers, so a store gets compared against stores that resemble it — plus the COD-market data (Egypt/KSA/UAE payment mix, RTO rates) that Western benchmark reports don't cover.

## 🎯 When I use it (real scenario)

This is the triage layer before any [CRO teardown](../prompts/cro/landing-page-cro-teardown.md): funnel-stage benchmarks tell me WHERE a store is abnormal before the teardown finds out WHY. And for COD stores, the payment-mix and RTO data here is the reality check I keep having to make — my own [COD Profit Funnel](../frameworks/cod-profit-funnel.md) framework exists because delivered revenue and dashboard revenue are different numbers, and now there's public data to put ranges on that gap.

## 📋 The Benchmarks

### 1 · Sitewide conversion — pick your panel first

| Panel | Conversion rate | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Shopify stores (SMB skew) | **1.4% median** · top 20% ≥3.2% · top 10% ≥4.7% | [Littledata, ~12,000 stores, 2025](https://www.littledata.io/average/ecommerce-conversion-rate) |
| Global average (UK mid-market skew) | 1.7% (down from 1.76%) | [IRP Commerce, 2025](https://www.irpcommerce.com/ecommercemarketdata.aspx) |
| Enterprise brands | ~3% (2.87–3.11% by device) | [Dynamic Yield/Mastercard XP², 2025-26](https://marketing.dynamicyield.com/benchmarks/) |
| Trend | overall CR fell 6.2% YoY; new-customer CR −7.4% | [Contentsquare, 90B sessions, 2025](https://contentsquare.com/press/2025-digital-experience-benchmarks/) |

By industry (Dynamic Yield): Food & Beverage **5.29%** (highest) · Pet Care up to 7.15% peak · Luxury & Jewelry **0.7%** (lowest). Food/pet converts 3-5x higher than luxury/electronics — category gravity is real; don't fight it with CRO expectations from another vertical.

### 2 · By device & traffic source

| Cut | Rate | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Desktop | 3.81% | [Contentsquare 2025](https://contentsquare.com/guides/digital-experience-benchmark/conversions/) |
| Mobile | 2.03% (desktop converts ~1.7-1.9x mobile) | same |
| Mobile share of retail traffic | ~76-77% | Contentsquare + Dynamic Yield, 2025 |
| Email traffic | ~4-5.3% (highest converting source) | [Eightx compilation, 2026](https://eightx.co/blog/average-ecommerce-conversion-rate-by-traffic-source-2026) ⚠️ agency aggregation — directional |
| Organic search | ~2.7-3.0% | same |
| Paid social | ~0.7-1.2% | same |

The structural insight: three-quarters of traffic is mobile and mobile converts half as well — mobile checkout friction is almost always a bigger prize than another desktop-first redesign.

### 3 · Funnel stages

| Stage | Benchmark | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Add-to-cart rate | 4.6% Shopify median (top 20% >11.5%) · 5.98% enterprise avg | [Littledata](https://www.littledata.io/average/add-to-cart-rate) · [Dynamic Yield](https://marketing.dynamicyield.com/benchmarks/add-to-cart-rate/) |
| Checkout completion (started → order) | **45%** Shopify avg · top 20% = 59% · top 10% = 66% | [Littledata, 2025](https://www.littledata.io/average/checkout-completion-rate) |
| Cart abandonment | **70.2%** global (aggregate of ~50 studies) | [Baymard Institute, 2025](https://baymard.com/lists/cart-abandonment-rate) |
| Recoverable via checkout UX alone | +35% average CR uplift possible | [Baymard checkout research](https://baymard.com/research/checkout-usability) |

**Why carts die (Baymard, US survey 2024-25):** extra costs too high (shipping/fees) **48%** — #1 for six straight years · just browsing 43% · checkout too long 18% · preferred payment missing 13%. The shipping-fee reveal remains the single most fixable leak in e-commerce.

### 5 · AOV (Dynamic Yield XP², 2025-26)

Luxury $389 · Home & Furniture ~$264 · Fashion $151-289 · Beauty ~$72 · Pet Care $64. By region: EMEA $193 > Americas $158 > APAC $125. Desktop AOV runs +37% over mobile. [Source](https://marketing.dynamicyield.com/benchmarks/average-order-value/)

### 6 · COD & MENA markets (the data Western reports skip)

| Metric | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| MENA COD preference trend | halved **41% → 20%** of consumers in 48 months; KSA/UAE/Kuwait as low as ~10% | [Checkout.com 4th MENA report, 2024](https://www.checkout.com/newsroom/checkout-coms-4th-annual-mena-report-finds-cash-on-delivery-usage-halved-amongst-maturing-digital-economy) |
| Egypt COD share of e-commerce payment value | ~45% (2024) — shopper stated preference 55-70% | [Mordor Intelligence](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/egypt-ecommerce-market) · [Consultancy-me, 2024](https://www.consultancy-me.com/news/11138/the-rise-of-online-shopping-and-ecommerce-in-egypt) |
| COD RTO rate (India — best published proxy) | **25-30%** of COD orders fail vs 2-8% prepaid; RTO rises 22%→35% as delivery slips from 1-2 to >5 days | [Shipway ShipNotes, 2024](https://mediabrief.com/shipnotes-reveals-26-rto-rate-on-cod-orders-across-india/) |
| UAE COD RTO | 15-20% (secondary sources); healthy target <12-15% | [eGrow guide, 2026](https://www.egrow.com/en/blog/the-complete-guide-to-reducing-return-to-origin-rto-in-cod-e-commerce-2026) ⚠️ weak sourcing |
| MENA e-commerce GMV | $34.5B (2024, +13%) → $57.8B by 2029 | [EZDubai report via DC360, 2025](https://www.digitalcommerce360.com/2025/05/27/mena-ecommerce-market-57-billion-by-2029/) |
| BNPL penetration | UAE 39%, KSA 42% of consumers | [Checkout.com MENA 2024](https://www.checkout.com/guides-and-reports/the-state-of-digital-commerce-in-mena-2024) |
| MENA first-attempt delivery success | **no reliable public benchmark exists** — carriers don't publish; track your own | — |

⚠️ Egypt COD figures range 34-90% depending on methodology (payment-value share vs stated preference vs social listening). Use the payment-value figure (~45%) for financial modeling; the RTO delivery-speed curve is the operational headline — every day of delivery delay measurably grows returns.

### 7 · Social proof (the canonical study)

Displaying reviews (5+) lifts conversion up to **+270%**; higher-priced products +380%; purchase likelihood peaks at **4.2-4.5 stars, not 5.0**; returns diminish sharply after the first ~5 reviews. [Spiegel Research Center, Northwestern, 2017](https://spiegel.medill.northwestern.edu/how-online-reviews-influence-sales/) — dated but never replicated at that rigor since.

## 🧠 How to use benchmarks

- **Panel-match or don't compare.** A Shopify SMB comparing itself to Dynamic Yield's enterprise 3% will chase a phantom; the honest Shopify question is "am I above the 1.4% median and climbing toward 3.2%?"
- **Stage rates beat sitewide rates**: sitewide CR hides which stage leaks. Benchmark ATC, checkout-completion, and abandonment separately — then run the [Funnel Decomposition Skill](../skills/funnel-decomposition/SKILL.md) on the gap.
- **COD stores: extend the funnel.** Everything above stops at "order"; your business stops at delivered cash. Apply the [COD Profit Funnel](../frameworks/cod-profit-funnel.md) rates on top.
- **The 48% shipping-cost stat is an instruction**: show total cost before checkout. It has been the #1 abandonment reason for six consecutive years — it will be next year too.
